AI & Marketing Technology

The technology race that will reshape how patients find care — and how we market it.

HEALTHCARE AI — NATIONAL

OpenAI, Amazon, Microsoft, and Epic all launched major clinical and consumer health AI products this quarter — and HCA is already live on ChatGPT for Healthcare

The healthcare AI market moved faster in the first quarter of 2026 than in all of 2024 combined. OpenAI launched ChatGPT Health on January 7 — a consumer-facing, encrypted health guidance tool where users connect their medical records — and followed immediately with ChatGPT for Healthcare, an enterprise platform running on GPT-5. Early hospital adopters include HCA Healthcare, Cedars-Sinai, Stanford Medicine Children's Health, and AdventHealth. Amazon launched Health AI for One Medical members on January 22, which books appointments, manages prescriptions, and routes patients to providers through in-app messaging or video visits — then expanded it to all U.S. Amazon customers on March 10. Microsoft launched Copilot Health on March 12, connecting users' electronic health records with wearable data. Epic unveiled Agent Factory at the Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society conference — a no-code platform letting health systems build their own AI agents — and reported that more than 85% of its customers now use Epic artificial intelligence. More than 40 million Americans already ask ChatGPT health questions daily, according to OpenAI.

Marketing Impact

HCA is already using ChatGPT for Healthcare. Are we? When a patient in Kendall asks ChatGPT which hospital to go to for cardiac surgery, that AI tool's answer is shaped by the data it has been trained on and the health system partnerships it has forged. HCA is already in that room. If Baptist Health is not actively exploring enterprise health AI partnerships and investing in our digital content so that AI tools surface us accurately and favorably, we are ceding patient acquisition to systems that move faster. This is not a 2027 priority — it is a 2026 conversation for leadership.

BH Impact

Sources: Becker's Hospital Review

MARKETING AI — ACTIONABLE

Epic's Agent Factory and ambient AI documentation tools are live — health systems that use them gain a measurable patient experience and retention advantage

Epic has released three named artificial intelligence agents now available to Epic customer health systems: Art, which documents patient visits and generates discharge summaries in real time; Penny, which handles prior authorization and revenue cycle coding; and Emmie, a patient-facing assistant for scheduling, lab result explanations, and general health questions. The Agent Factory platform lets health systems deploy their own custom agents without writing code. Advocate Health in Charlotte is already using it. Additionally, Epic's artificial intelligence charting tool, which listens during patient visits and drafts clinician notes automatically, launched February 4. These tools reduce physician administrative burden, improve throughput, and free clinicians to spend more time with patients — directly affecting patient experience scores that drive online reputation and word-of-mouth referrals.

Marketing Impact

If Baptist Health is on Epic, we should be piloting these tools now. The physician documentation burden is one of the top drivers of clinician dissatisfaction and rushed patient visits — both of which translate directly to lower patient experience ratings and weaker referral networks. An ambient documentation pilot across one service line (cardiology or orthopedics, high-volume and high-value) would let us quantify the impact and generate a compelling physician recruitment story. 'Baptist Health doctors spend more time with patients because AI handles the paperwork' is a positioning line that resonates with both patients and the physicians we are trying to recruit.

AI MARKETING TOOLS — ACTIONABLE

AI-powered patient acquisition tools are now clinic-grade — not just enterprise-grade — narrowing the technology gap between systems and independent competitors

The 2026 healthcare marketing technology landscape has fractured in a way that benefits large health systems willing to move fast. AI-powered tools for personalized patient outreach, appointment conversion, and search-engine-optimized content generation are now widely available and have moved from experimental to operational. Health systems using AI-driven search engine marketing and content personalization are reporting measurable lifts in appointment conversion rates and reduced cost-per-acquisition. At the same time, independent practices and urgent care chains are adopting the same tools, narrowing the technology advantage that enterprise marketing teams once held. The differentiation is no longer access to the technology — it is the quality of the strategy behind it.

Marketing Impact

Our technology advantage is eroding — strategy is the new moat. The marketing team should audit which AI-powered tools we are actively using for patient acquisition, content optimization, and campaign personalization. If the answer is fewer than three, we are behind. The question for leadership is not whether to invest in AI marketing tools — it is which ones, with which data assets, aimed at which patient populations. A quarterly AI marketing capability review should be standing agenda for the digital team.

POLICY — FEDERAL

The 'One Big Beautiful Bill' reconciliation package advances Medicaid work requirements — Florida's uninsured population could grow materially

The federal reconciliation legislation advancing through Congress includes Medicaid work requirements mandating that adults ages 19 to 64 enrolled in Medicaid expansion demonstrate 80 hours per month of qualifying activity — paid work, job training, or community service — or lose coverage. The KFF projects the 2025 reconciliation law will reduce federal Medicaid spending by an estimated $911 billion over 10 years, creating fiscal pressure on state Medicaid programs that will cascade into hospital reimbursement rates. Florida has not expanded Medicaid, but the retroactive coverage change (from 90 days to 30 or 60 days) would affect existing Florida Medicaid enrollees. Florida health justice advocates have published analysis warning that work requirements in other states failed to improve employment while increasing uninsured rates — and critics warn Florida could see similar outcomes. Florida's baseline uninsured rate is already among the highest in the nation.

Marketing Impact

More uninsured patients in South Florida means increased pressure on our uncompensated care load and payer mix. Our marketing strategy must stay laser-focused on commercially insured 18 to 64 patient acquisition — because that population's profitability funds the community care mission. At the same time, policy volatility creates an opportunity for BH to deepen its community trust positioning: a health system that fights for coverage access and provides financial counseling and charity care communicates values that resonate with the communities we serve, and that resonance translates to brand preference when those patients do have coverage.

BH Impact

Sources: Kaiser Family Foundation, Miami Herald, KFF Health News

POLICY — MEDICARE

New $2,100 Medicare Part D out-of-pocket drug cap provides real financial relief to Florida's senior population

Starting in 2026, Medicare Part D prescription drug coverage imposes a firm $2,100 annual out-of-pocket cap on covered prescription drugs. Once enrollees reach that threshold, their cost-sharing obligation ends for the year. For Florida — which has one of the nation's oldest and largest Medicare populations — this represents a meaningful improvement in medication affordability and adherence. Better drug adherence among senior patients translates to better management of chronic conditions, fewer emergency department visits, and higher patient satisfaction scores. Baptist Health's Medicare Advantage relationships and pharmacy services are positioned to benefit from this policy shift as it increases the relative value of comprehensive network care.

Marketing Impact

The Part D cap is a Medicare marketing moment. Florida seniors who previously rationed medications due to cost may now be more willing to engage with their full care plan — including specialist visits, preventive screenings, and chronic disease management programs that Baptist Health provides. A targeted Medicare patient communication campaign explaining the new drug benefit and connecting it to our comprehensive senior care services (concierge medicine, Baptist Health Quality Network) is a low-cost, high-relevance outreach opportunity this quarter.
