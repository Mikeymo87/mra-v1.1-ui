 AI & Marketing Technology
The artificial intelligence and marketing technology moves that will reshape patient acquisition and clinical operations
HEALTHCARE AI
Ambient artificial intelligence scribes have crossed the mainstream threshold — six major health systems are deploying them at scale

The American Hospital Association's Center for Health Innovation reported in April 2026 that six major health systems are now deploying ambient artificial intelligence documentation tools at scale, citing ambient clinical documentation as one of the most significant artificial intelligence use cases in healthcare settings. The tools automatically transcribe and structure clinical conversations into medical records, eliminating the manual documentation burden that drives physician burnout.

A Nature partner journal study published in 2026 found that ambient scribes are expanding beyond low-acuity settings into complex care environments, with mounting evidence for reduced documentation time, improved clinician-patient interaction, and decreased burnout rates. The study also flagged remaining accuracy challenges in complex or multilingual clinical conversations — directly relevant to South Florida's diverse patient population.

For BH specifically, the multilingual accuracy challenge is not theoretical. A significant portion of BH's patient base communicates primarily in Spanish or Haitian Creole. Ambient tools trained primarily on English-language clinical dialogue will underperform in these encounters without specific fine-tuning.

Marketing Impact

Physician satisfaction is a marketing asset. Physicians who spend less time on documentation talk about it — to colleagues, to trainees, to the media. If BH deploys ambient artificial intelligence tools and makes them part of the physician experience story, it becomes a recruitment and retention differentiator that competitors without the technology cannot easily replicate. Consider building ambient documentation adoption into BH's physician marketing narrative alongside the Sunrise hospital and FIU academic medical center stories — all three signal a future-ready organization.

MARKETING AI

AI WE ARE WATCHING
Virtual care marketing is no longer optional — systems that treat telehealth as a line item are losing the acquisition battle

The 2025-2026 healthcare marketing trend analysis from multiple sources converges on one point: virtual care is no longer a pandemic-era feature — it is a core patient acquisition channel. Health systems that market virtual care as a convenience add-on are underperforming against those that treat it as a gateway to long-term relationship care.

The specific opportunity: commercially insured patients ages 18-64 who first engage a health system through a virtual visit are significantly more likely to convert to in-person specialty and procedural care within 12 months than those who engage through urgent care. Virtual care is a funnel, not a destination — and health systems that market it as such are building more durable patient relationships.

Marketing Impact

BH's virtual care offerings should be front-and-center in primary care and urgent care marketing — not as a differentiator (every system has virtual care now) but as an on-ramp. The message is: 'Start virtual, get referred to the right specialist, stay with BH.' This is the care continuity story that identity marketing can own. Target the 25-44 commercially insured segment in particular — they are the highest virtual care adopters and the lowest BH brand loyalty cohort.

CRITICAL — National / Florida
The 2025 reconciliation law is now being implemented — Medicaid work requirements and coverage restrictions will hit Florida's patient population hard

The 2025 federal reconciliation law — signed July 4, 2025 — is now entering its implementation phase with direct consequences for South Florida's patient population. Key provisions being implemented now:

  • Medicaid work requirements take effect January 1, 2027, with states required to be operationally ready before then. Nebraska has already announced early enforcement starting May 1, 2026. Florida must prepare systems for the January 2027 deadline.
  • Coverage restrictions for lawfully present immigrants are already taking effect, with an estimated 1.4 million people nationally projected to lose coverage. In South Florida's Miami-Dade County, where more than 50 percent of residents are foreign-born, this restriction will be felt more acutely than in most markets.
  • Provider tax restrictions are immediate: states cannot establish new provider taxes or increase existing ones, eliminating a key Medicaid financing tool that public systems like Broward Health and Memorial Healthcare have historically relied on.
  • More frequent eligibility redeterminations for Medicaid expansion adults will generate coverage churn, temporarily pushing some eligible people off Medicaid even when they qualify for re-enrollment.

The Congressional Budget Office estimates $911 billion in reduced federal Medicaid spending over 10 years. While the largest financing cuts do not take effect until 2027 or later, the fiscal pressure is immediate for state budgets.

Marketing Impact

The payer mix math is shifting. As Medicaid rolls contract — through work requirements, immigrant eligibility restrictions, and coverage churn — some patients will lose coverage entirely, some will transition to marketplace plans, and some will remain covered. The patients who transition to marketplace plans become commercially insured patients. That transition creates a narrow acquisition window: patients who were Medicaid-insured and are now shopping for marketplace coverage are actively evaluating their healthcare options, often for the first time. BH should have targeted messaging and digital campaigns ready to reach newly marketplace-enrolled patients in Miami-Dade and Broward — the counties most affected by immigrant coverage restrictions and coverage churn. The message: BH accepts your new plan, here are our locations near you, here is how to establish care.

BH Impact
Sources: Kaiser Family Foundation, KFF — Reconciliation Bill Tracker, Healthcare Dive
WATCH — National / Florida
Enhanced Affordable Care Act premium tax credits are expiring — South Florida marketplace enrollment could contract sharply by end of 2026

The enhanced premium tax credits that expanded Affordable Care Act marketplace coverage during the pandemic era are set to expire at the end of 2025, with no congressional extension confirmed. Kaiser Family Foundation analysis projects that coverage losses will mount as the clock runs out, with lower-income marketplace enrollees facing premium increases they cannot afford once enhanced subsidies disappear.

In South Florida, where Affordable Care Act marketplace enrollment has been among the highest in the nation — driven by Florida's decision not to expand Medicaid — the expiration of enhanced credits hits a particularly large commercially insured population. Some enrollees will lose coverage; others will shift to lower-tier plans with higher deductibles and narrower networks, reducing care utilization.

Marketing Impact

BH's network participation in key Affordable Care Act marketplace plans is a marketing asset that needs to be actively communicated to at-risk enrollees. As enhanced credits expire and enrollees reconsider their plan choices, BH should be running plan selection season campaigns (open enrollment runs November through January) that position BH as accessible under the most widely available marketplace plans. Work with managed care contracting to confirm which marketplace plans BH participates in, then build digital campaigns targeting those plan members in advance of renewal decisions.
