Coverage Loss
The Coverage Cliff Is Here — Florida Is the Hardest-Hit State in the Nation

The Florida Policy Institute's analysis — updated August 2025 and now unfolding in real time — confirms that Florida faces the most severe healthcare coverage loss of any state under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act. The legislation, signed July 4, 2025, allows enhanced Affordable Care Act Marketplace premium tax credits to expire at end of 2025 and introduces new enrollment hurdles. Combined with Medicaid cuts, the Congressional Budget Office projects 1.5 million Floridians will lose health insurance — 1.4 million of them (93 percent) due specifically to the Marketplace changes.

Florida's uninsured rate is rising from a historic low of 10.7 percent in 2023 to a projected 16.7 percent in 2026 — levels not seen since before the Affordable Care Act. Non-expansion states like Florida are hit hardest: people earning 100 to 138 percent of the federal poverty level had relied on Marketplace subsidies but do not qualify for Medicaid. The Wakely consulting group projects 53 to 64 percent coverage loss among Marketplace participants in non-expansion states. South Florida is cited specifically as one of the most acutely affected regions in the state.

The uncompensated care burden for Florida hospitals is projected to reach $5.2 billion annually — the highest of any state. This will stress every system in our market, particularly those serving lower-income communities.

Marketing Impact

For BH marketing, the near-term implication is a sharpening of focus on the commercially insured 18-64 population — those who retain insurance through employer-sponsored plans. This group is becoming more scarce and more valuable simultaneously. BH's brand positioning as the premium, trusted health system for people who have choices — and who make intentional choices about their care — is exactly right for this environment. Every campaign dollar should be evaluated against its ability to reach and convert this population.
